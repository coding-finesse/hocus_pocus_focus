## The "Ghost" Sub

A streaming app sees a 20% spike in sign-ups, but 80% cancel after the 7-day free trial.

**The Ask:**  
Do you require credit card info upfront to "filter" users, or simplify the app to increase retention?  

**The Risk:**  
Asking for a card kills sign-ups by 50%.  

---

## Answer

If 100 original → 120 sign-ups post spike with 80% cancellations → 24 users.  
Requiring a credit card would cut sign-ups to 60 (50% drop from 120).

**Fix:**  
Skip the credit card as it kills trust. Use Apple Pay / Google Pay / UPI for low friction, and switch to a **freemium** model with a subscription tier.

Retaining 120 users (credit card −50% and end‑of‑trial −80% is off the table) beats forcing 60 users to commit upfront.  
Keep basic services free and offer premium add‑ons.

Don’t just keep the 120 users: monetize them by using ads for free‑tier users to cover running (server) costs, while pushing them toward premium.  

Less friction + more throughput.
