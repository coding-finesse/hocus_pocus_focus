### Case Study: The Profit Leak

**Scenario:**
An entrepreneur opens a coffee shop. 
* It’s always full of customers.
* The coffee is priced fairly.
* The staff is efficient.

**The Problem:** 
At the end of the month, the shop is losing money. What’s the first thing you investigate to find the leak?

---

**Analysis & Point of View:**
*   **Efficiency vs. Popularity:** If the shop is full but losing money, the issue is not popularity but operational efficiency[cite: 1].
*   **Cost of Goods & Overheads:** Money may be leaking through order preparation costs, high rent, or service expenses like fans, AC, and lighting[cite: 1].
*   **Order Volume:** Because individual orders are typically designed to be profitable, the total volume of orders might be too low compared to the number of people in the shop[cite: 1].

**Key Areas to Investigate:**
1.  **Table Occupancy:** Identify if customers are placing low-value orders but staying for a long time, or if small groups are occupying large tables for extended periods[cite: 1].
2.  **MRP & Profitability:** Ensure the pricing strategy (Maximum Retail Price) is structured to be highly profitable[cite: 1].
3.  **Operations & Wastage:** Look for misses in service, staff behavior issues, or significant product wastage[cite: 1].

*Note: Order volume often needs a spike to offset fixed costs when occupancy is high but revenue remains low[cite: 1].*
